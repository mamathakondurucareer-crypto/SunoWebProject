=== SONG TITLE ===
Raghupati Raghava — Song of Praise

=== LYRICS ===
[Verse 1]
Raghupati Raghava Raja Ram
Patita Pavana Sita Ram
Bolo bolo sab mil bolo
Ram naam satya hai

[Chorus]
Ram naam ki mahima nirali
Har dil mein basi hai
Ek hi naama ek hi dham
Sita Ram Sita Ram

[Verse 2]
Ishwar Allah tero naam
Sabko sanmati de Bhagwan
Antar ke andhiyaare mein
Jyoti jalaao Ram

[Chorus]
Ram naam ki mahima nirali
Har dil mein basi hai
Ek hi naama ek hi dham
Sita Ram Sita Ram

[Bridge]
Dohe ke sangam mein
Tere naam ka jaap hai
Har pal har ghadi
Teri bhakti ka khwaab hai

[Outro]
Siya Ram Siya Ram
Siya Ram jay jay Ram

=== STYLE NOTES ===
Devotional bhajan with roots in Tulsidas bhakti tradition. Tempo: 72 BPM. Key of Sa/G major. Instrumentation: harmonium (lead), tabla (rhythm), sarangi (sustain), light manjira. Mood: warm, reflective, triumphant by the bridge. Vocal texture: soulful lead with layered choir harmonies entering at the chorus.

=== VOCAL GUIDANCE ===
Emphasise "Ram" and "Sita" in verse 1 with a gentle gamak. The word "mahima" in the chorus should be held for two beats on a rising interval. Breathe after "andhiyaare" in verse 2. Bridge should build dynamically — slight crescendo into "bhakti ka khwaab". Pronunciation note for the Outro: "Siya" — stress the first syllable, SI-ya. Close vowels on "Ram" and "dham" at the end for resonance.

=== BACKGROUND ===
Raghupati Raghava Raja Ram is one of the most beloved bhajans in the Vaishnava bhakti tradition, composed in devotion to Lord Ram. This arrangement draws on Tulsidas's Ramcharitmanas while adding a contemporary chorus that reflects the transforming power of Ram Nam — the divine name that purifies and liberates. The bhajan is traditionally sung during morning aarti and Ram Navami celebrations across Bharat.
